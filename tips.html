Ways to Reduce Screen Time:

  • Set daily limits for your device usage.
  • Take regular breaks when using devices.
  • Avoid using screens before sleeping.
  • Turn off unnecessary notifications

Healthy habits:

  • Spend more time outdoors.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Try hobbies like reading, drawing, or sports.
  • Spend more time with family and friends.

For families and schools:

  • Parents should set rules for screen use.
  • Create device-free times (for example during meals).
  • Encourage children to balance screen time with other activities.
  • Schools can educate students about healthy technology use.

Practical Strategies:

  • Use apps to track and limit screen time.
  • Create a daily routine with limited screen use.
  • Keep devices out of bedrooms.
  • Replace screen time with useful or active tasks.

Benefits of reducing screen time

Reducing screen time has many important benefits for both physical and mental well-being. It can lead to better sleep and increased energy levels, as less exposure to screens—especially at night—helps the body rest properly. It also improves focus and concentration, making it easier to complete tasks and stay attentive. In addition, spending less time on screens can support better mental health and mood by reducing stress and overstimulation. It also allows for stronger real-life relationships, as people have more time to connect face-to-face with others. Finally, reducing screen time can boost productivity in school or work, helping individuals manage their time more effectively and achieve better results.
